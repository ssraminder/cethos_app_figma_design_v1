## Part B — Records Retention & Trial-Document Storage
**Retention period.** Long-term archival of clinical-trial essential documents (the Trial Master File — retained **≥ 25 years** under EU Regulation 536/2014, plus ICH E6 / FDA requirements) is the **sponsor's** responsibility. As a service provider, Cethos retains **trial / COA project content only for the duration of the engagement**, then **returns it to — or securely deletes it for — the sponsor** per the contract. Cethos retains **its own quality and project records** (qualification, QC, validation, audit, and the service record) for **at least 7 years**, or longer where a sponsor contract specifies. Where a contract sets a specific vendor-retention period, that period applies.

**Ownership.** Trial documents remain the property of the **sponsor / client**; Cethos holds them as a processor and returns or securely deletes them per the contract on completion.

**Storage.** Trial / COA content is stored only in the **four controlled, certified storage systems** recorded in REG-SP-001 — Supabase, AWS S3 (backup), Microsoft 365 / SharePoint, and Dropbox — encrypted at rest and in transit, access-controlled (SOP-014), and backed up (SOP-016).

**Protection & integrity.** Records are protected from loss and unauthorised alteration; regulated audit-trail records are append-only (21 CFR Part 11). Backups and a tested recovery capability apply (SOP-016 / SOP-017; CTS-REC-RST-002).

**Disposal.** At the end of the retention period, or per contract, records are securely deleted and the disposal is recorded.
