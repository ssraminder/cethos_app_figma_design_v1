## 1. Purpose and Scope
This Quality Policy establishes the foundation for the Quality Management System at Cethos Translation Services (12537494 Canada Inc.) and demonstrates senior management's commitment to quality excellence in all services provided to clients in the pharmaceutical and medical device industries. It applies to all personnel, processes, and services delivered by Cethos, including linguistic validation, translation, cognitive debriefing, clinician reviews, and related clinical research support services.

## 2. Company Overview
Cethos Translation Services is a Calgary-based clinical research services provider specializing in linguistic validation and clinical services for pharmaceutical and medical device companies conducting global clinical trials. Cethos operates with a **core internal team** across linguistic operations, vendor management, account management, and finance, supported by an **on-roll translator team** and a network of 640+ vetted specialist linguists across 95+ languages for translation services and 150+ languages for clinical services. **Cognitive Debriefing and Clinician Review services are delivered through a documented partnership (established 2020)** operating under the Cethos QMS.

## 3. Quality Policy Statement
Cethos Translation Services is committed to providing the highest quality linguistic validation and clinical research services to the pharmaceutical and medical device industries. We recognize that the quality of our work directly impacts patient safety, data integrity, and regulatory compliance in clinical trials worldwide. We achieve our quality objectives through:
- **Adherence to International Standards** — ISO 17100 for translation services, ISO 9001 quality-management principles, ISPOR guidelines for linguistic validation, and ICH Good Clinical Practice (GCP).
- **Rigorous Quality Control** — every deliverable undergoes independent QC review by qualified specialists before submission, with documented quality checkpoints throughout.
- **Qualified Personnel** — all translators, moderators, and medical professionals undergo comprehensive qualification including credential verification, proficiency assessment, therapeutic-area evaluation, and GCP training where applicable.
- **Continuous Training and Development** — ongoing training for internal staff and external resources to maintain current knowledge of standards, therapeutic areas, and quality requirements.
- **Client Focus** — open communication, prompt responses, proactive issue escalation, and continuous feedback.
- **Data Security and Confidentiality** — protection of client and participant data through secure systems, access controls, confidentiality agreements, and compliance with PIPEDA and GDPR where applicable.
- **Process-Based Management** — operations governed by documented SOPs, regularly reviewed and updated.
- **Systematic Problem Resolution** — root-cause analysis, effective corrective and preventive actions, and effectiveness verification.
- **Continuous Improvement Culture** — all personnel are encouraged to identify improvement opportunities.

## 4. Quality Objectives
Our quality objectives are measurable, achievable, and aligned with our strategic goals, monitored regularly and reviewed during management reviews:
- **Client Satisfaction** — 95%+ satisfaction; respond to inquiries within 24 hours; 85%+ repeat business.
- **Delivery Performance** — 98%+ on-time delivery; meet agreed milestones; proactively communicate potential delays.
- **Quality of Deliverables** — client-reported errors below 2%; first-pass acceptance 95%+; 100% QC review before submission.
- **Compliance and Training** — 100% of required training completed annually; GCP certification maintained for clinical-services personnel; zero major audit findings.
- **Vendor Management** — qualified vendor network with 95%+ performance ratings; qualification completed before assignment; annual performance reviews.
- **Continuous Improvement** — annual internal audits of all critical processes; 95% of corrective actions closed on time; at least three significant process improvements annually.

## 5. Management Responsibility and Commitment
Senior management demonstrates commitment to quality through leadership and communication, resource allocation, quarterly management reviews, and by ensuring the Quality Manager has the authority and independence to halt non-conforming work, report directly to senior management, and initiate corrective actions. The Quality Manager role is currently held on an acting basis by the Founder & CEO, with compensating controls per SOP-003. The organization fosters a culture in which quality is everyone's responsibility, errors are learning opportunities, and client needs drive decisions.

## 6. Policy Communication and Review
This Quality Policy is communicated to all personnel through orientation and annual refresher training, posting on internal systems, and distribution to vendors during qualification. It is reviewed annually or when significant changes occur (organizational restructuring, regulatory changes, client feedback, strategic changes, or audit findings). Revisions are documented, approved by senior management, and communicated to affected personnel.
