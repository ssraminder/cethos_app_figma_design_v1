## 1. Introduction and Scope
### 1.1 Purpose
This Quality Manual describes the Quality Management System (QMS) implemented at Cethos Translation Services (12537494 Canada Inc.). It is the primary reference for all quality-related activities and demonstrates our commitment to delivering high-quality linguistic validation and clinical services to the pharmaceutical and medical device industries.

### 1.2 Scope
This Quality Manual applies to all services provided by Cethos Translation Services, including translation and linguistic validation, back translation and reconciliation, harmonization, cognitive debriefing and patient interviews, clinician reviews and expert consultations, and quality assurance and quality control services.

### 1.3 Company Overview
Cethos Translation Services is a Calgary-based clinical research services provider specializing in linguistic validation and clinical services for pharmaceutical and medical device companies conducting global clinical trials. Cethos operates with a **core internal team** spanning linguistic operations, vendor management, account management, and finance, supported by an **on-roll translator team** and a network of 640+ vetted specialist linguists across multiple therapeutic areas. **Cognitive Debriefing and Clinician Review services are delivered through a documented partnership** (see §1.4 and §3). Language coverage spans 95+ languages for translation services and 150+ languages for clinical services.

### 1.4 Strategic Partnerships
Cethos maintains strategic relationships with major industry players including TransPerfect, RWS Group, WeLocalize, Acolad, and FACIT. In addition, Cethos maintains a **documented partnership, established in 2020, for the delivery of Cognitive Debriefing and Clinician Review services**, operating under the Cethos QMS, through which qualified consultants are engaged and managed (see §3).

## 2. Quality Policy
### 2.1 Quality Policy Statement
Cethos Translation Services is committed to providing the highest quality linguistic validation and clinical services to the pharmaceutical and medical device industries. We achieve this through adherence to international quality standards including ISO 17100, ISO 9001, and ISPOR guidelines; compliance with Good Clinical Practice (GCP) principles; continuous improvement of our processes and services; investment in staff training and development; rigorous vendor qualification and performance monitoring; and protection of participant confidentiality and data security. The full Quality Policy is maintained as QP-001.

### 2.2 Quality Objectives
Our quality objectives are measurable, achievable, and aligned with our strategic goals: achieve 95% or higher client satisfaction; maintain on-time delivery of 98% or higher; reduce client-reported errors to less than 2% of deliverables; complete 100% of staff training requirements annually; and conduct internal audits of all critical processes annually.

### 2.3 Management Commitment
Senior management demonstrates commitment to quality through active participation in management reviews, allocation of adequate resources, establishment of quality objectives, and promotion of a culture of continuous improvement throughout the organization.

## 4. Quality Management System Overview
### 4.1 QMS Framework
The Cethos QMS is designed to meet ISO 17100 for translation services, ISO 9001 for quality management, ISPOR guidelines for linguistic validation, and GCP principles for clinical research activities, integrating process-based management, risk-based thinking, and continuous improvement.

### 4.2 Process Approach
The QMS is built on management processes (strategic planning, management review, resource management), core processes (translation, back translation, reconciliation, cognitive debriefing, clinician reviews), support processes (document control, training, vendor management, IT infrastructure), and monitoring processes (quality control, internal audits, performance metrics, client feedback).

### 4.3 Documentation Hierarchy
QMS documentation is structured in four levels: Level 1 — Quality Manual (this document); Level 2 — Standard Operating Procedures (registered in QM-002); Level 3 — Work Instructions; Level 4 — Forms and Records.

### 4.4 Regulatory Compliance
Cethos maintains compliance with applicable regulations and guidelines including ICH GCP, FDA guidance for patient-reported outcomes, EMA guidelines on translation and linguistic validation, Health Canada regulations, and applicable data-protection laws including PIPEDA.

## 5. Document Control and Records Management
### 5.1 Document Control System
All QMS documents are controlled so that only current, approved versions are in use. Each controlled document carries a unique identifier, version number, approval signatures, effective date, and revision history. Controlled SOPs are maintained in the Cethos portal SOP registry, which enforces versioning and is immutable after approval.

### 5.2 Document Approval and Distribution
New documents and revisions require approval by the document owner, the Quality Manager, and senior management as appropriate. Approved documents are distributed through the secure document-management system, with notifications to affected personnel.

### 5.3 Document Review and Revision
SOPs and work instructions are reviewed at least annually or when processes change. Changes are tracked through revision-history logs; obsolete documents are archived and clearly identified as superseded.

### 5.4 Records Management
Quality records provide evidence of conformity and effective QMS operation, including project files, training records, audit reports, CAPA documentation, vendor qualification files, and client correspondence. Records are legible, identifiable, retrievable, and protected. Retention periods comply with contractual and regulatory requirements, typically a minimum of 7 years for clinical research projects.

### 5.5 Electronic Records and Signatures
Electronic documents and records are managed with appropriate access controls, audit trails, backup procedures, and compliance with 21 CFR Part 11 where applicable (see CSV-001 / CSV-002).

## 6. Training and Competency Management
### 6.1 Training Program Overview
Cethos maintains a training program ensuring all personnel are competent for their tasks. Training needs are identified from job roles, regulatory requirements, process changes, and performance gaps.

### 6.2 Internal Staff Training
New employees receive orientation covering company policies, QMS overview, GCP principles, data protection and confidentiality, information security, and role-specific responsibilities. Ongoing training includes annual GCP refreshers, SOP training on revision, therapeutic-area training, technology updates, and quality-awareness training.

### 6.3 Vendor Training and Qualification
All freelance translators, moderators, and medical professionals undergo qualification before assignment, including credential verification, reference checks, sample-work evaluation, GCP training for clinical roles, confidentiality-agreement execution, and therapeutic-area assessment (SOP-003 / SOP-019).

### 6.4 Training Documentation
Training records are maintained for all personnel, including topic and date, trainer, trainee acknowledgement, assessment results where applicable, and materials version. Records are reviewed during internal audits and management reviews.

### 6.5 Competency Assessment
Competency is assessed through written assessments, practical evaluations, work-product review, client feedback, and peer review, and is reassessed when performance issues arise or significant process changes occur.

## 7. Supplier and Vendor Management
### 7.1 Vendor Network
Cethos maintains a network of 640+ qualified specialists including translators specializing in medical and pharmaceutical terminology, cognitive-debriefing moderators, medical professionals across therapeutic areas, and QA specialists.

### 7.2 Vendor Qualification Process
All new vendors undergo a rigorous qualification process — application review, initial assessment, training and onboarding, trial project, and approval — governed by SOP-003, with the COA-specific path in SOP-019.

### 7.3 Vendor Performance Monitoring
Vendor performance is monitored using quality, on-time delivery, responsiveness, adherence to guidelines, client feedback, and error rates. Performance data is reviewed, and vendors not meeting standards are placed on improvement plans or removed.

### 7.4 Vendor Database Management
The vendor database maintains contact details, language pairs and specializations, therapeutic-area expertise, certification and training records, performance metrics, availability, and rate information, with access restricted to authorized personnel.

### 7.5 Requalification
Vendors are requalified every 12 months or when performance issues are identified, including updated credential verification, current CV review, additional training, and reassessment of proficiency or clinical knowledge.

## 8. Project Management and Client Communication
### 8.1 Project Initiation
Projects begin with comprehensive planning including client-requirements review, scope definition, resource allocation, timeline development, risk assessment, and quality-requirements identification, confirmed with the client at kick-off.

### 8.2 Project Planning
Detailed project plans cover work-breakdown structure, resource assignments, milestone schedule, quality checkpoints, communication plan, and risk mitigation, documented and approved before execution.

### 8.3 Client Communication
Regular client communication includes status reports, milestone notifications, prompt responses, proactive issue escalation, and check-in meetings, with a single point of contact per project.

### 8.4 Change Management
Changes to scope, timeline, or deliverables are managed through a formal change-control process: documented, impact-assessed, client-approved, and incorporated with team notification.

### 8.5 Project Closure
On completion, deliverables are submitted and approved, final invoicing completed, project files archived, lessons learned documented, and client satisfaction assessed, with a closure report for internal review.

## 9. Linguistic Validation Processes
### 9.1 Translation Process
Forward translation follows ISPOR guidelines with dual forward translations by independent native-target translators with relevant expertise, a translator reconciliation meeting, and a preliminary translated version.

### 9.2 Back Translation
Back translation is performed by native-source translators who have not seen the original, are independent from the forward translators, and hold medical-translation credentials.

### 9.3 Reconciliation
Reconciliation compares forward and back translations, resolves discrepancies, addresses cultural appropriateness, and creates a reconciled translation, documented with rationale.

### 9.4 Harmonization
For multi-country studies, harmonization ensures consistency across regional variants, with common terminology, cultural adaptation, and resolution of regional differences.

### 9.5 Quality Control
All translations undergo independent QC by a qualified specialist, checking conceptual equivalence, grammatical accuracy, clarity, adherence to style guides, glossary consistency, and formatting; findings are documented and addressed before delivery.

### 9.6 Formatting and Finalization
Final deliverables are formatted to client specifications, including structure, headers/footers, page numbering, fonts and spacing, and clean track-changes documentation.

## 10. Clinical Services Processes
### 10.1 Cognitive Debriefing Overview
Cognitive debriefing tests translated instruments with target patient populations to ensure translations are comprehensible, culturally appropriate, and conceptually equivalent. These services are delivered through the documented partnership (§3.4) under the Cethos QMS.

### 10.2 Protocol Development
Each study begins with a protocol specifying inclusion/exclusion criteria, sample size and recruitment, interview methodology, question guide, data-collection procedures, and analysis approach, reviewed and approved by the client.

### 10.3 Participant Recruitment
Participants are recruited through qualified channels ensuring appropriate demographics, relevant clinical characteristics, native fluency, and informed-consent compliance, per local regulations and ethics.

### 10.4 Interview Conduct
Interviews are conducted by trained moderators who are native speakers, experienced in qualitative research, knowledgeable about the disease area, and GCP-certified, following the approved protocol and guide.

### 10.5 Data Analysis and Reporting
Interview data is analyzed to identify comprehension issues, cultural concerns, suggested modifications, and confirmation of conceptual equivalence, with reports including demographics, item-level findings, recommendations, and anonymized supporting quotes.

### 10.6 Clinician Reviews
Clinician reviews ensure medical accuracy and clinical appropriateness, performed by licensed healthcare professionals with relevant specialty expertise and native target fluency, delivered through the documented partnership (§3.4).

### 10.7 Confidentiality and Ethics
All clinical activities maintain strict confidentiality including participant anonymization, secure data storage, limited access, and compliance with applicable privacy laws.

## 11. Quality Control and Quality Assurance
### 11.1 Quality Control Activities
QC activities are embedded throughout, including in-process checks, independent QC review of all deliverables, proofreading, verification of formatting and completeness, and client review cycles (SOP-010).

### 11.2 Quality Assurance Program
The QA program provides independent oversight through audits of processes and projects, review of metrics and trends, vendor-performance assessment, training-effectiveness evaluation, and process-improvement recommendations, led by the Quality Manager.

### 11.3 Quality Metrics
Key metrics tracked include on-time delivery, client satisfaction, error rates by type and severity, vendor-performance ratings, training completion, CAPA closure times, and audit findings, reviewed in management reviews.

### 11.4 Client Feedback
Client satisfaction is assessed through post-project surveys, periodic account reviews, inquiry responses, and repeat-business analysis, used to drive improvement.

### 11.5 Error Classification and Trending
Errors are classified by type, severity, and root cause; trending identifies systemic issues requiring corrective action.

## 12. Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA)
The CAPA process (SOP-011) systematically identifies, investigates, and resolves quality issues while preventing recurrence, covering issue identification, root-cause analysis, corrective and preventive actions, effectiveness verification, and central tracking. All personnel are empowered to report issues.

## 13. Internal Audits
Cethos conducts internal audits (SOP-012) to verify compliance with SOPs and regulatory requirements, assess QMS effectiveness, identify improvements, and prepare for external audits, on an annual schedule covering all critical processes, with documented findings and follow-up to closure.

## 14. Management Review
Senior management conducts formal QMS reviews at least quarterly (SOP-013), considering quality metrics, client feedback, audit results, CAPA status, vendor performance, training effectiveness, regulatory changes, and resource needs, producing decisions and actions that are documented and tracked.

## 15. Risk Management
Cethos employs a proactive, risk-based approach (SOP-015): risks are identified, assessed for likelihood and impact, mitigated through avoidance/reduction/transfer/acceptance, and monitored, with risk registers maintained and updated.

## 16. Data Security and Confidentiality
Cethos maintains comprehensive information-security measures (SOP-014) covering access controls, encryption, confidentiality agreements, secure data handling, physical security, and incident response. Backup and recovery are governed by SOP-016 and business continuity by SOP-017. Part 11 / computerized-system controls are assessed in CSV-001 / CSV-002.

## 17. Regulatory Compliance
Cethos maintains compliance with applicable regulations and standards including ICH-GCP, ISO 17100, ISO 9001, ISPOR good practices, and data-protection laws (PIPEDA, GDPR, HIPAA principles where applicable). Regulatory changes are monitored and incorporated into the QMS.

## 18. Continuous Improvement
Cethos fosters a culture of continuous improvement, identifying opportunities from metrics, audits, client feedback, employee suggestions, benchmarking, and technology, and implementing enhancements with appropriate training and documentation.
