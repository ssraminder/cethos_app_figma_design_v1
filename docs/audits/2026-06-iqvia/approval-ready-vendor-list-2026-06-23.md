## Method
Of **894** active translator applications, only **14** clear the full ISO 17100 §6.1.2 bar enforced by the (newly fixed) reviewer guide: a **passed test or quiz** + a **verified degree (route a) or references confirming ≥5 yrs (route c)** + **≥1 reference** + a **current, linked NDA**. The other ~880 are "→ Not yet" (no completed assessment, unverified/absent basis, or no NDA) and must not be approved. The SMOKE-TEST dummy (APP-26-9900) is excluded — it only qualifies because of test data I created.

## Per-vendor approval steps (for each row)
1. Open the profile → confirm the reviewer-guide verdict.
2. **Verify the basis:** route (a) — open the degree doc, confirm it's a translation/relevant degree; route (c) — confirm the reference(s) corroborate ≥5 yrs. Record the §3.1.4 basis.
3. **Trim the listed high-risk domains** (no domain-specific evidence — general competence doesn't cover them per §6.1.6). Keep one only if you open a domain-specific certificate that covers it.
4. Click **Approve**.
