## Read this first — what "ready" does and does NOT mean
Everyone in this queue has cleared two gates automatically:
- **Competence demonstrated** — passed the translation test (≥1 approved combo) or the quiz (≥80%).

It does **NOT** mean ISO-qualified. **`qualification_basis` is blank for all 31** — establishing it is the reviewer's job. For each applicant you must confirm the four items below and **record the §3.1.4 basis** when you approve.

## The 4 checks for EVERY applicant (ISO 17100 §3.1.4 / §6.1)
1. **Competence** — open the test/quiz result; confirm it's a genuine pass, not a borderline/fluke. (Be extra careful where the **AI prescreen score is low but the test passed** — read the AI red flags on the prescreen card.)
2. **§3.1.4 qualification basis — pick ONE and verify it from the CV:**
   - **(a) Translation/linguistics degree** — confirm the **field** (the system only stores the *level*, not the field — you must read the CV).
   - **(b) Degree in another field + ≥2 years** documented translation experience.
   - **(c) ≥5 years** documented professional translation experience (no degree required).
3. **Reference** — read the received reference; confirm it's credible and supports the basis. Policy: **2 requested, approve on 1 if it's good for ISO.**
4. **Domain scope (§6.1.6)** — approve **only the domains that are evidenced** (tested + CV/portfolio). Do **not** rubber-stamp every self-declared domain — several applicants declared 10–20 domains; certified/medical/pharma/legal claims need evidence.

**Also:** confirm a **CV is on file** and an **NDA** is signed (NDA is part of onboarding — chase if missing).
